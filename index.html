Days Until We're Not Apart:

I'm always there.

To: Mildred, Margaret, & Felicia

From: Handler

Our Poems

Mine

Time has cheated us
When did it steal the hours
I’ve never felt less anxious
You opening up to me like the morning flowers

Finally disconnected from the world
My worries archived for later by your gaze
Me in the center, your head curled
Do you have to leave with the morning rays

Everything is silent
Only feeling your heartbeat
My responsibilities are so distant
Our encounters never are complete

One more for the road
The drive is quite long
Until we meet for the next episode
I’ll feel as if something is wrong

Yours

For just one day, perhaps,
We will choose to ignore the alarm.
Let its screeches be ambient noise,
For it can do us no harm.

For just this day, perhaps,
We will until the afternoon stay,
Let the day pass by without us,
While tomorrow is still far away.

For just this morning, perhaps,
We will watch sunlight trickle in,
Fill the room with light that is golden
And your face with a peaceful grin.

But for this morning, however,
We must drag ourselves out of bed.
There are things that demand our attention, so
For that morning we must still look ahead.
